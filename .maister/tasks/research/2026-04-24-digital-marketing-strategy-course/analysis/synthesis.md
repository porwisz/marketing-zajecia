# Research Synthesis: Digital Marketing Strategy Course (8-Hour Day)
## Cross-Source Analysis, Patterns, and Key Insights

**Research question**: How to design and prepare a comprehensive 8-hour lecture-workshop day on Digital Marketing Strategy and Execution for postgraduate e-marketing students, covering SOSTAC, RACE, Digital Strategy, Brand Strategy, and Customer Value Proposition — with current market data, case studies, a Keynote presentation with presenter notes, and Canva Whiteboard exercises?

**Synthesis date**: 2026-04-24
**Sources integrated**: 5 findings files (frameworks-models-theory, market-data-statistics, case-studies-international, polish-market-cases, course-design-facilitation)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Five distinct research streams converge into a coherent, high-quality course design. The theoretical foundation (SOSTAC + RACE + CVP + Brand Strategy) is well-documented from primary sources. Market data is current, rich, and immediately usable. Ten case studies (5 international, 5 Polish) provide full framework-mapped examples with hard metrics. Course design best practices are evidence-based and directly applicable. The synthesis reveals four dominant patterns: (1) all frameworks form a deliberate hierarchy, not a flat set of tools; (2) Polish examples are as analytically strong as international ones and in many cases more practically relevant for this audience; (3) the 70/30 activity-to-lecture ratio is well-supported by both andragogy research and the volume of practical material available; and (4) the Canva Whiteboard exercises can be sequenced to mirror the framework hierarchy, creating a single integrated learning arc across the day.

One significant gap exists: no verified Polish digital advertising spend data (IAB Polska AdEx report) was accessible during research. This is the only data hole of consequence.

### 2.2 Contradictions Identified and Resolved

**Contradiction 1: YouTube reach figures for Poland**
- DataReportal reports 29.0 million advertising reach (75.6% of population)
- Gemius/PBI Mediapanel reports 24.4 million monthly real users
- Resolution: These measure different things. DataReportal counts advertising reach (impressions-based); Gemius counts unique monthly visitors authenticated against panel data. Both are valid for different use cases. For a course slide, the Gemius figure (24.4M) is more conservative and defensible for audience size claims; use DataReportal for advertising reach planning discussions.

**Contradiction 2: SOSTAC pedagogical order vs. logical hierarchy**
- Logically, Brand Strategy is upstream of SOSTAC (you need to know who you are before you plan)
- The course-design file places Brand Strategy/CVP in the afternoon, after SOSTAC and RACE in the morning
- Resolution: These reflect different logics — conceptual hierarchy vs. pedagogical sequencing. Teaching SOSTAC first gives students a concrete planning scaffold, after which the more abstract brand and CVP concepts become meaningful because students understand where they fit in the plan. Teach explicitly: "We are learning SOSTAC first because it is the most actionable tool — but in practice you would define your brand strategy before filling in SOSTAC."

**Contradiction 3: Nike CDA success vs. subsequent revenue decline**
- Nike's DTC strategy succeeded by metrics ($21.3B NIKE Direct revenue, 160M NikePlus members)
- Nike's total revenue then fell to $46.3B in FY2025 (-9.84%) as the company began re-engaging wholesale partners
- Resolution: Not a contradiction but a teaching opportunity illustrating the RACE framework's Reach stage trade-off. Nike's over-rotation to DTC built Engage excellence but created a Reach deficit. Both phases of the case are valuable. Use this to teach that RACE stages are not independent — optimizing one at the expense of another creates systemic risk.

## 3. Patterns and Themes

### Pattern 1: The Framework Hierarchy (Architectural)

All five frameworks form a clear upstream-to-downstream hierarchy. Brand Strategy is foundational and long-term. Digital Strategy extends it to the organization. Digital Marketing Strategy focuses it on the marketing function. SOSTAC structures the planning process. RACE structures the execution across the customer lifecycle.

Evidence: Explicit hierarchy diagram in frameworks-models-theory (primary source); CVP described as "brand expression" and embedded within SOSTAC Strategy stage; RACE described as the operational layer of SOSTAC Tactics and Actions. Prevalence: central to all framework relationships and supported across all case studies.

Teaching implication: This hierarchy should anchor the entire day's narrative — open with the diagram, return to it at every module transition. It prevents the common student failure of treating SOSTAC and RACE as disconnected alternatives.

### Pattern 2: Product = Marketing (Implementation)

The most successful case studies show companies where the product itself is the primary marketing vehicle, reducing the need for and cost of paid advertising.

Evidence:
- Spotify Wrapped: the personalization product IS the annual marketing campaign
- Duolingo: the streak mechanic IS the brand narrative on TikTok
- InPost: the Paczkomat network density IS the brand message ("7 minutes from home")
- CD Projekt Red: free DLC and DRM-free policy ARE the PR strategy and brand differentiator
- Żabka: Nano autonomous stores ARE the brand story and earned media driver

Prevalence: Clearly present in 5 of 10 cases; partially present in the remaining 5. Quality: Strong, consistent across both international and Polish markets.

Teaching implication: Powerful counter-narrative to "marketing as separate from product." Connect directly to CVP theory — when a product's design directly eliminates customer pains and creates customer gains, word-of-mouth and earned media replace paid acquisition.

### Pattern 3: Data as Competitive Moat (Design)

The most defensible digital competitive advantages in the case studies are first-party data assets, not technology platforms or brand awareness alone.

Evidence: Spotify's behavioral data enables personalization that creates switching costs in a commodity market. Zalando's 51M-user dataset enables both consumer personalization and B2B brand intelligence services. Żabka's Żappka loyalty data enables hyper-personalization that increases visit frequency. mBank's digital-first model since 2000 means richer customer behavioral data than branch-based competitors. Market data corroborates: 93% of marketers say personalization improves conversions; yet only 13% use advanced techniques.

Teaching implication: Use this pattern in SOSTAC Situation Analysis to teach competitive advantage assessment. Ask: "What first-party data assets does this brand have that competitors lack?"

### Pattern 4: Omnichannel as Mutual Compensation (Architectural)

"Omnichannel" in the best-performing cases is not "website + store" — it is designing digital and physical experiences so each compensates for the other's specific limitations.

Evidence: IKEA AR removes the visualization gap that blocked online furniture purchases. Żabka Nano uses the digital Żappka app as the physical door-unlock mechanism. InPost Paczkomat is physical infrastructure whose entire value chain is digital (app, notifications, tracking). Polish market data confirms: 81% of Polish online shoppers prefer parcel lockers; 84% say convenient lockers increase their online shopping frequency.

Teaching implication: When teaching RACE Act and Convert stages, use omnichannel examples to show that digital and physical touchpoints should be designed as one integrated system.

### Pattern 5: Polish Market Specificity (Organizational)

Poland has several market-specific digital phenomena that require separate treatment from global frameworks: BLIK as dominant payment method (68% of e-commerce), InPost Paczkomat as infrastructure standard (88% locker market share), Allegro as quasi-monopoly marketplace (45–50% e-commerce market share), and a demographic shift ("silver tsunami") where the largest social media age group is now 55–75 year olds.

Evidence: All five Polish cases, market-data-statistics Poland section, polish-market-cases Part 2.

Teaching implication: When teaching digital channel strategy in Poland, these market specifics change recommendations significantly. Platform prioritization, delivery integration, and payment UX recommendations for Poland differ substantially from global best practices.

## 4. Key Insights

### Insight 1: The Framework Hierarchy Solves the Day's Narrative Arc Problem

A common failure mode in one-day marketing workshops is a disconnected series of topics that feel independently interesting but leave students unable to integrate them. The Brand Strategy → Digital Strategy → SOSTAC → RACE hierarchy provides a single logical spine. Each module answers a successively more operational question: "Who are we?" → "What does digital transformation mean for our organization?" → "How do we plan our digital marketing?" → "How do we execute it across the customer lifecycle?" Opening with this hierarchy and returning to it at each transition prevents the "why are we learning this?" question that undermines engagement.

Confidence: HIGH. Supported by frameworks-models-theory primary sources and course-design facilitation best practices.

### Insight 2: Polish Cases Provide Superior Pedagogical Value for This Audience

For postgraduate students in Poland, the Polish cases offer higher engagement, contextual relevance, and application potential than most international cases. Students can verify claims from their own experience (they have used Paczkomat, they have seen Allegro ads, they bank with mBank). However, international cases demonstrate scale, global platforms, and cross-cultural strategic thinking that Polish cases cannot fully replace. Recommended weighting: Polish cases as primary workshop exercises; international cases as lecture examples and comparative analysis.

Confidence: HIGH (pedagogical principle corroborated by andragogy research on "prior experience" as adult learning driver).

### Insight 3: Single-Brand Exercises Are Superior to Multi-Brand Exercises

If each exercise uses a different brand, students build four disconnected half-finished analyses. If exercises use the same brand throughout the day, outputs build into a near-complete digital marketing plan — a tangible artifact that demonstrates SOSTAC-RACE integration. This mirrors how SOSTAC is used in professional practice. The facilitator should encourage groups to choose one brand in the morning warm-up and apply all four exercises to that brand.

Confidence: HIGH. Directly supported by course-design-facilitation (SOSTAC as "backbone for a full semester-long project") and adult learning principles (applicability and immediate relevance).

### Insight 4: Market Statistics Should Be Embedded as Evidence, Not Front-Loaded as Data

A "statistics dump" slide at the start of the day is ineffective — numbers without strategic context are quickly forgotten. The strongest statistics should appear at the moment they provide persuasive evidence for a strategic decision students are actually making. For example: the TikTok buyer conversion rate (43.8%) is most powerful when placed alongside a channel planning decision about whether to include TikTok in a Reach strategy. Email ROI ($36–50 per $1 spent) is most powerful when placed in a RACE Engage module about retention channels.

Confidence: HIGH. Supported by course-design facilitation visual principles and adult learning "problem orientation" principle.

### Insight 5: The CVP Canvas Is the Best Cross-Framework Integration Exercise

The Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder) forces students to complete Customer Profile (Jobs, Pains, Gains) before building the Value Map — which mirrors the correct logical order for SOSTAC Situation Analysis (understand customers first) and for Brand Strategy (brand promise must address real customer needs). Running the VPC exercise after students have already mapped a brand's RACE stages means they come to the canvas with concrete knowledge of how the brand currently reaches and converts customers — making the "gaps and opportunities" phase of the exercise genuinely analytical rather than speculative.

Confidence: HIGH. Supported by frameworks-models-theory (CVP Canvas methodology) and course-design-facilitation (exercise sequencing logic).

### 5.1 Framework Dependency Map

```
Brand Strategy (who are we? — long-term, relatively stable)
        |
        v
Digital Strategy (how do digital technologies transform our organization?)
        |
        v
Digital Marketing Strategy (how do we use digital to attract/convert/retain?)
        |
        v
SOSTAC Planning Process:
  S (Situation) → audit: customers, competitors, company, PESTEL
  O (Objectives) → SMART targets per RACE stage
  S (Strategy) → targeting, positioning, channel priorities
  T (Tactics) → mapped to RACE execution framework
  A (Actions) → owners, timelines, budgets (4Ms)
  C (Control) → KPI dashboards per RACE stage
        |
        v (Tactics/Actions layer)
RACE Execution Framework:
  Reach (Awareness) → SEO, PPC, social, content, influencer
  Act (Consideration) → landing pages, email, content, lead magnets
  Convert (Purchase) → CRO, checkout UX, retargeting, offers
  Engage (Loyalty) → email retention, loyalty programmes, NPS, advocacy
        |
        v (at each stage)
Channel Mix + KPI Selection + Budget Allocation
```

CVP is cross-cutting: it is defined in Brand Strategy, expressed in SOSTAC Strategy (positioning), tested in RACE Convert, and validated in RACE Engage.

## 6. Gaps and Uncertainties

### 6.1 Confirmed Data Gaps

1. **IAB Polska AdEx 2024 channel-by-channel breakdown**: IAB Polska website was not accessible during research due to TLS certificate errors. The 9.2B PLN total digital ad spend figure is available via secondary sources, but breakdown by channel (search/social/video/display) was not verified from primary source. Facilitator should obtain the Raport Strategiczny IAB Polska before the course.

2. **Polish influencer marketing spend in PLN**: Only global figures available ($24B globally). Polish-specific spend data should be sourced from IAB Polska report or native Polish marketing research.

3. **AI marketing tool adoption rates in Poland specifically**: The 89% global AI tool adoption figure (CMI) may not translate to the Polish market. Polish-specific data not found.

4. **Current 2026 Polish digital campaigns**: Research was conducted in April 2026. The facilitator should add 1–2 examples of campaigns running in Q1-Q2 2026 for maximum relevance (check marketingprzykawie.pl, Marketer+, Social Press).

### 6.2 Where Facilitator Should Supplement

- If cohort is B2B-heavy: add B2B RACE examples (LinkedIn, gated content, lead scoring, account-based marketing) — not deeply covered in this research
- Student industry context: the warm-up exercise surfaces what industries students work in; facilitator should be prepared with 1–2 industry-specific SOSTAC examples
- Platform pricing in PLN: current Meta Ads, Google Ads CPM/CPC benchmarks in Poland for budgeting exercises

### 6.3 Unresolved Inconsistencies

LinkedIn reach discrepancy in Poland: DataReportal reports 8.20M advertising reach; Gemius reports approximately 5M monthly users. The gap (3.2M) is larger than for other platforms and may reflect international LinkedIn users accessing from Polish IPs, or methodological differences. Both figures should be presented with source context in slides rather than as definitive facts.

## 7. Primary Conclusions

1. **The day has a natural architecture**: Brand Strategy + CVP (foundation) → SOSTAC (planning process) → RACE (execution framework) → Case Studies + Market Data (evidence embedded throughout). This architecture should be explicit in both the agenda and the Keynote deck from the first slide.

2. **Polish cases are first-class teaching materials, not supplements**: For this specific audience (Polish postgraduate e-marketing students), Polish cases are primary. Allegro's brand storytelling, InPost's CVP design, Żabka's loyalty flywheel, CD Projekt Red's community marketing, and mBank's digital-first pioneer story are more contextually relevant than most international equivalents and are analytically equivalent in framework illustration depth.

3. **Single-brand exercises produce better learning outcomes**: Running all four Canva Whiteboard exercises on one brand per group is pedagogically superior and produces a near-complete digital marketing plan as the day's artifact.

4. **Market data should be embedded in strategic moments, not front-loaded**: Statistics have maximum persuasive power when they appear at the point in the day where students are making the strategic decision the data informs.

5. **The only critical content gap is current 2026 Polish campaign examples**: Everything else — frameworks, data, cases, exercises, design principles — is comprehensively covered in this research package. The facilitator needs to add one thing: fresh examples from the current market.
