# Research Plan: Digital Marketing Strategy Course — 8h Workshop Day

## Research Overview

### Research Question
How to design and prepare a comprehensive 8-hour lecture-workshop day on Digital Marketing Strategy and Execution for postgraduate e-marketing students, covering SOSTAC, RACE, Digital Strategy, Brand Strategy, and Customer Value Proposition — with current market data, case studies, concrete digital examples, a Keynote presentation with presenter notes, and Canva Whiteboard exercises with facilitator descriptions?

### Research Type
Mixed — combines:
- **Literature research**: established frameworks (SOSTAC, RACE, CVP, Brand Strategy), adult learning pedagogy
- **Content research**: 2024-2025 market data, real-world case studies, digital campaign examples
- **Requirements research**: 8-hour session design, workshop facilitation, Keynote and Canva Whiteboard deliverable structure

### Scope and Boundaries

**In scope**:
- SOSTAC and RACE model theory, components, and practical application
- Digital strategy vs. brand strategy distinction
- Customer Value Proposition frameworks (Value Proposition Canvas and alternatives)
- Brand strategy fundamentals: positioning, architecture, brand promise
- 2024-2025 digital marketing statistics (ad spend, mobile, social, e-commerce)
- Case studies: Polish brands (Allegro, Żabka, CD Projekt, InPost, Empik) and international brands (IKEA, Nike, Zalando, Spotify, Duolingo)
- 8-hour hybrid lecture+workshop agenda with timing and transitions
- Canva Whiteboard exercise design with facilitator instructions
- Keynote slide structure with presenter notes guidance
- Adult learning principles applicable to postgraduate mixed-experience audiences

**Out of scope**:
- Coding, analytics platform setup, technical implementation
- Deep SEO/SEM tactics beyond strategic overview
- Paid media buying mechanics
- Social media content production workflows

**Constraints**:
- Language: Polish
- Duration: exactly 8 hours including breaks
- Audience: postgraduate e-marketing students, mixed experience
- Tools: Keynote + Canva Whiteboard
- Deliverable format: Keynote with presenter notes + Canva Whiteboard exercises with facilitator guide

---

## Methodology

### Primary Approach
Multi-source literature and content synthesis:
1. Gather authoritative definitions and frameworks from academic and practitioner sources
2. Collect current (2024-2025) market data from industry reports (Statista, IAB, Google, Meta, Gemius/PBI for Poland)
3. Identify and document case studies with enough detail for classroom discussion
4. Apply instructional design principles (Kolb's experiential learning cycle, andragogy) to structure the 8-hour day
5. Map all content to a Keynote slide outline and identify natural points for Canva Whiteboard exercises

### Fallback Strategies
- If specific Polish-market data is unavailable, use CEE (Central and Eastern Europe) regional data and note the caveat
- If a case study lacks sufficient public documentation, substitute with a comparable brand in the same category
- If exact slide counts are unclear, define sections with time budgets and let the presenter subdivide

### Analysis Framework

| Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Framework completeness | Are SOSTAC, RACE, CVP, and Brand Strategy covered with enough depth for 90-120 min each? |
| Data currency | Is all market data from 2024 or 2025? Are sources citable? |
| Case study richness | Does each case study have: background, strategy, execution, result, and 2-3 discussion questions? |
| Pedagogical flow | Does the day follow a logical arc from theory to application? Are workshops anchored to prior theory? |
| Polish relevance | Is at least 40% of examples and data Poland-specific or CEE-specific? |
| Deliverable readiness | Can the Keynote outline and Canva exercise descriptions be directly used by a designer/facilitator? |

## Research Phases

### Phase 1: Broad Discovery (Frameworks and Models)
**Goal**: Establish accurate, citable definitions and process steps for all five content pillars.

**Actions**:
- Fetch authoritative SOSTAC model description: components, worked example, common pitfalls
- Fetch RACE model description: components, integration with SOSTAC, KPIs per stage
- Fetch Value Proposition Canvas: components (customer jobs, pains, gains; value map), worked example
- Fetch brand strategy fundamentals: positioning statement formula, brand architecture types, brand promise definition
- Fetch digital strategy definition and how it differs from marketing strategy and brand strategy
- Capture Kolb learning cycle and andragogy principles for pedagogical justification

**Expected output**: Definitions, diagrams descriptions, component lists, worked examples for each framework

---

### Phase 2: Targeted Data Collection (Market Statistics)
**Goal**: Gather specific, citable 2024-2025 statistics to integrate into the presentation as supporting data.

**Actions**:
- Retrieve global digital ad spend figures and growth rate (2023-2025)
- Retrieve Polish digital ad spend total and channel breakdown (search, social, display, video)
- Retrieve mobile internet usage share in Poland and globally
- Retrieve e-commerce penetration and growth data for Poland and Europe
- Retrieve social media platform usage statistics relevant to Poland (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn)
- Retrieve key consumer behavior shifts: ROPO effect, mobile-first purchasing, subscription model growth

**Expected output**: A structured data table with statistic, value, year, and source for each data point

---

### Phase 3: Case Study Deep Dive
**Goal**: Build 5-7 well-documented case studies usable as classroom discussion material.

**Actions**:
- For each brand (Allegro, Żabka, Nike, Zalando, Spotify + 1-2 more): gather background, strategic challenge, applied digital/brand strategy, specific tactics/channels, measurable outcomes, and 2-3 discussion questions
- Identify which framework each case study best illustrates (e.g., Allegro → SOSTAC application; Spotify → CVP and brand personality)
- Note the source URL and publication year for each case study fact

**Expected output**: 5-7 case study cards with structured fields: Brand, Challenge, Strategy, Execution, Result, Frameworks Illustrated, Discussion Questions, Sources

---

### Phase 4: Course Design and Structure
**Goal**: Produce a complete 8-hour agenda and design specifications for all workshop exercises and Keynote sections.

**Actions**:
- Design the 8-hour day structure: opening, 4-5 content blocks, workshop slots, breaks, closing — with exact time allocations
- Map each content block to the relevant framework and case study
- Design at least 3 Canva Whiteboard exercises: objective, instructions for participants, facilitator debrief questions, estimated time
- Define Keynote presentation structure: sections, approximate slide counts per section, types of slides (theory, data, case study, transition)
- Write presenter notes guidance: what the presenter says vs. what appears on screen, transition cues, timing warnings
- Apply andragogy principles: relevance anchoring, experience activation, self-direction, immediate application

**Expected output**: Full agenda table, 3+ exercise specifications, Keynote outline with section-by-section slide plan

---

### Phase 5: Verification and Gap Check
**Goal**: Ensure all success criteria from the research brief are met and identify any remaining gaps.

**Actions**:
- Cross-check all 8 success criteria against gathered findings
- Verify that at least 3 case studies have complete discussion questions
- Verify that all market data is from 2024 or 2025 and has a citable source
- Verify that at least 3 Canva Whiteboard exercises have full facilitator instructions
- Identify any framework or data gap and note it explicitly in the findings
- Flag any area where data confidence is low (e.g., if only indirect Polish data is available)

**Expected output**: Gap analysis checklist, confidence ratings per section

## Gathering Strategy

### Instances: 5

| # | Category ID | Focus Area | Tools | Output Prefix |
|---|-------------|------------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | frameworks-models | SOSTAC, RACE, CVP, Brand Strategy theory; adult learning pedagogy | WebSearch, WebFetch | frameworks-models |
| 2 | market-data | Global and Polish digital marketing statistics 2024-2025; ad spend, mobile, e-commerce, social | WebSearch, WebFetch | market-data |
| 3 | case-studies | International brand case studies: Nike, Zalando, Spotify, Duolingo, IKEA | WebSearch, WebFetch | case-studies |
| 4 | polish-market | Polish brand case studies and market context: Allegro, Żabka, InPost, CD Projekt; Polish digital ecosystem | WebSearch, WebFetch | polish-market |
| 5 | course-design | Workshop design principles, 8-hour session structure, Canva Whiteboard facilitation, Keynote structure, instructional design | WebSearch, WebFetch | course-design |

### Rationale
Five parallel gatherers are chosen because the five focus areas have low overlap and require distinct search strategies:
- **Frameworks-models** requires authoritative academic and practitioner sources with precise definitions — mixing this with data collection would reduce precision.
- **Market-data** requires structured numerical data retrieval from industry reports — a dedicated gatherer can be systematic about completeness and source credibility.
- **Case-studies** and **polish-market** are separated because Polish sources require different search terms, languages, and credibility assessment (local vs. international press). Keeping them separate ensures neither crowd out the other.
- **Course-design** covers instructional design and facilitation methodology — a distinct domain from marketing content — and benefits from focused attention on pedagogy rather than subject matter.
