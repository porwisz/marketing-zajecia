# Research Brief: Digital Marketing Strategy Course — 8h Workshop Day

## Research Question

How to design and prepare a comprehensive 8-hour lecture-workshop day on Digital Marketing
Strategy and Execution for postgraduate e-marketing students, covering SOSTAC, RACE,
Digital Strategy, Brand Strategy, and Customer Value Proposition — with current market data,
case studies, concrete digital examples, a Keynote presentation with presenter notes, and
Canva Whiteboard exercises with facilitator descriptions?

## Research Type

**Mixed** — requires literature research (models, frameworks, best practices), requirements
research (course design pedagogy, timing, adult learning), and content research
(market data, case studies, real-world examples).

## Scope

### Included
- SOSTAC model: theory, components (Situation, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Actions, Control), application in digital marketing
- RACE model: theory, components (Reach, Act, Convert, Engage), integration with SOSTAC
- Digital strategy definition, purpose, and differentiation from brand strategy
- Brand strategy fundamentals: brand architecture, positioning, brand promise
- Customer Value Proposition (CVP): definition, frameworks (e.g., Value Proposition Canvas), examples
- Current market data: digital ad spend, mobile usage, social media stats, e-commerce trends (2024-2025)
- Real-world case studies: brands that applied digital strategy successfully (e.g., IKEA, Nike, Zalando, Allegro, Żabka)
- Concrete digital marketing examples: campaigns, channel strategies, KPIs
- 8-hour timing and session structure (intro, breaks, workshops)
- Workshop exercise design: Canva Whiteboard activities with step-by-step instructions for facilitator
- Keynote presentation structure: slide flow, visual design guidance, presenter notes

### Excluded
- Technical implementation (coding, analytics platform setup)
- Paid media buying mechanics beyond strategic overview
- Deep SEO/SEM tactics (only high-level strategic context)
- Social media content production

### Constraints
- **Language**: Polish (the course is delivered in Polish)
- **Duration**: Exactly 8 hours (including breaks)
- **Audience**: Postgraduate students in e-marketing, mixed experience levels
- **Format**: Hybrid lecture + workshop
- **Tools**: Keynote (presentation) + Canva Whiteboard (exercises)
- **Deliverables**: Keynote with presenter notes + Canva Whiteboard exercises with facilitator guide

## Success Criteria

1. Complete coverage of all required models (SOSTAC, RACE) with practical application frameworks
2. At least 3 concrete case studies with discussion questions
3. Current market data (2024-2025) integrated into presentation
4. Full 8-hour agenda with timing, transitions, and break structure
5. At least 3 Canva Whiteboard exercises with full facilitator instructions
6. Keynote slide outline (section by section) with presenter notes guidance
7. Introduction covering Digital Strategy, Brand Strategy, and CVP with clear distinctions
8. Facilitation tips and discussion prompts for key topics

## Classification

- **Research type**: Mixed (literature + requirements + content)
- **Primary sources**: External (web research for models, data, case studies), internal (problem statement)
- **Expected confidence level**: High (well-established models with abundant external sources)
